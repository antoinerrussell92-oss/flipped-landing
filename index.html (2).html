The problem

The lease office is designed against you.

Dealers have the rebate sheet. They have the money factor. They have the tier matrix. They have the scripts. Until now, you had nothing.

The money factor markup
The base money factor is on the manufacturer's rebate sheet. Dealers mark it up 1–2 points and you'd never know — it doesn't show as a line item. On a $38,500 lease going from 0.00200 to 0.00300 that's $60/month extra — $2,176 over the term.
The residual sandbagging trick
A lower residual means a higher payment. Dealers sometimes present a residual below the published rate — especially early in negotiations. The difference can be $30–$50 per month on a $40K vehicle.
The tier classification game
Many manufacturers treat Tier 1, 2, and 3 identically for lease rates — but dealers tell customers they're "only Tier 2" to justify a higher money factor. Most customers accept it without question.
The backend product sweep
The finance office pitch — GAP, extended warranty, paint protection, wheel and tire — is where the real profit lives. On a lease, most products are either redundant or useless. You need to know which ones.
